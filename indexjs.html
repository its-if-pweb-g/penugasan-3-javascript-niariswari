Bad Genius

Rate 9.5/10

Honestly, I only got around to watching this film in 2024, even though it was released in 2017. I hadn’t seen any spoilers or reviews beforehand, so I didn’t know what to expect. But once I started watching, I was hooked! The movie was full of suspense and kept me on the edge of my seat. The story’s clever take on academic cheating and the intense situations really made me think. "Bad Genius" is a thrilling and thought-provoking film that I’d recommend, especially for students.

A Man Called Otto

Rate: 9.5/10

This film takes you on an emotional journey, beginning with a heavy sadness and slowly moving towards hope. While the story deals with serious themes, it includes heartwarming moments that make you smile. Otto, the main character, seems tough on the outside but is actually quite vulnerable and relatable. The film shows how the people around us can have a big impact on our lives, often in ways we don’t immediately notice. It made me think deeply about friendship, love, and the possibility of new beginnings.

Coraline

Rate: 8.7/10

This film has been one of my favorite animated films since childhood. Even though it’s in the dark fantasy horror genre, it focuses more on excitement than fear. The film features charming animation and unique visuals, creating an atmosphere that’s a bit spooky but still fun. Its story about courage and self-discovery also carries a meaningful message. It’s one of my comfort movies, always entertaining and captivating, especially when I’m bored.
